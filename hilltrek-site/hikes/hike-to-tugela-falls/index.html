Hike to Tugela Falls

The world's highest waterfall, from below. Royal Natal's amphitheatre, the Berg's most iconic skyline, and one of the most photographed walks in Southern Africa.

The route

Tugela Falls drops a total of 948 m off the lip of the Drakensberg amphitheatre in five tiers — making it the world's highest waterfall on the latest measurement. The walk-in from the Royal Natal side traces the Tugela river gorge upstream, weaving through indigenous forest patches, river crossings, and the looming wall of the amphitheatre overhead the whole way.

You don't need to summit to make this hike worth it. The middle section — where the gorge opens up and the amphitheatre is fully in frame — is the shot every Berg photo book has tried to capture. It earns the cliché.

What worked

  • Early start — the trail gets busy by mid-morning, and the light is best before 10:00
  • Trail shoes with grip — the river crossings get slick and the chain-ladders demand confident footwork
  • Carrying more water than feels necessary — the gorge bakes by midday
