Firemaple Hiking Kettle

Lightweight, durable, and pack-friendly. The kettle that has shown up on every Berg outing we can remember without complaining.

Hilltrek verdict

Pair it with the Fire Force stove and you've got a boil-water-fast system that handles everything from morning coffee to freeze-dried dinners. Easy recommend.

Why we trust it

A kettle on a hiking trip is one of those items that either delights or annoys - and this one delights. The shape is right for nested storage with our cookset, the lid stays put, and the handle folds flat without complaining.

We've boiled litres of mountain water in this thing over the years. It still looks the same. That's the test that matters.

What it does well

  • Light enough to barely notice in the pack
  • Pours cleanly - no dribbling all over the camp table
  • Lid clamps stay tight when the kettle is full
  • Nests with most Firemaple cooksets

Watch-outs

  • Aluminium build means handle gets hot - bring a buff or pot grabber
  • No insulated lid - heat loss is real if you don't drink fast
